Churros II

Churros are a type of fried dough pastry that originated in Spain and are now popular throughout many countries in Latin America and around the world. They are typically made from a simple dough of flour, water, and salt, which is piped through a star-shaped nozzle and then fried until crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Churros are often served with a dipping sauce, such as chocolate or dulce de leche, and can be enjoyed as a snack or dessert. They are commonly served in long, straight sticks or in a coiled shape, and may be dusted with sugar or cinnamon for added flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 cup water
  • ½ cup margarine
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 3 eggs
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the water, butter, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium-high heat until the mixture comes to a boil and the butter is fully melted.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and add the flour to the pan. Stir vigorously with a wooden spoon until the dough comes together and pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  3. Remove the pan from the heat and let it cool for 5-10 minutes. Add the eggs one at a time, stirring vigorously after each addition until the dough is smooth and glossy.
  4. Heat a large pot or deep fryer filled with vegetable oil over medium-high heat until it reaches 375°F (190°C).
  5. Transfer the churro dough to a pastry bag fitted with a large star tip. Pipe the dough directly into the hot oil, cutting off the dough with scissors or a knife once it reaches your desired length (typically 4-6 inches or 10-15 cm).
  6. Fry the churros until they are golden brown on all sides, turning occasionally with tongs to ensure even cooking. Remove the churros from the oil with a slotted spoon and transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  7. If desired, coat the churros in cinnamon sugar while they are still warm.
  8. Serve the churros with your favorite dipping sauce, such as melted chocolate, caramel, or dulce de leche.
